🇮🇳 The 8 States — From Gujarat to Mizoram

The Tropic of Cancer enters India at the western border of Gujarat and exits at the eastern border of Mizoram. Memorising the sequence from west to east helps you recall all eight.

1 Gujarat 2 Rajasthan 3 Madhya Pradesh 4 Chhattisgarh 5 Jharkhand 6 West Bengal 7 Tripura 8 Mizoram

🌡️ Why the Tropic of Cancer Matters — Climate, Agriculture & Energy

The Tropic of Cancer is not just a line on the map — it is a climatic boundary that fundamentally shapes India's geography.

  • Tropical vs. Subtropical: The area south of the Tropic of Cancer lies in the tropical zone, where temperatures remain relatively high year‑round and the monsoon is the dominant climatic influence. The area north of the line experiences a subtropical climate with more pronounced seasonal variation — cold winters in Punjab, Haryana, and the Himalayan foothills.
  • Agriculture: Crop patterns shift across this line. South of it, tropical crops like rice, coconut, rubber, and spices thrive. North of it, wheat, mustard, and temperate fruits dominate. The Kharif‑Rabi cropping cycle is directly influenced by the latitudinal position relative to the Tropic.
  • Solar Energy: Regions along and south of the Tropic of Cancer receive the highest solar insolation in India — the amount of solar radiation reaching the Earth's surface. This makes Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh ideal for large‑scale solar power projects. India's National Solar Mission targets many of its largest installations in this belt.
  • The Summer Solstice: On 21 June, the Sun appears directly overhead at the Tropic of Cancer — this is the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere. The city of Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh, lies almost exactly on the Tropic, and its ancient Jantar Mantar observatory was built in part to track this precise astronomical event.

📊 Management‑School Context — Geography as a Business Variable

For anyone in agriculture, renewable energy, logistics, or retail, the Tropic of Cancer is a business variable. A company planning cold‑chain logistics for perishable goods must understand that southern India has different temperature profiles than northern India. A solar energy developer must know that the highest insolation zones are along this belt. A retailer sourcing mangoes must know that the tropical south produces different varieties and has different harvest windows than the subtropical north.

The Tropic of Cancer also passes through some of India's most mineral‑rich states — Gujarat (petrochemicals, ports), Madhya Pradesh (copper, diamonds), Chhattisgarh (coal, iron ore), Jharkhand (minerals). Understanding this geography means understanding where India's industrial resources are concentrated, and why certain states are economic powerhouses while others are not.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: Through how many Indian states does the Tropic of Cancer pass? Name them.+

The Tropic of Cancer passes through 8 Indian states: from west to east — Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Tripura, and Mizoram.

Q2: What is the climatic significance of the Tropic of Cancer for India?+

It divides India into tropical (south) and subtropical (north) climatic zones. South of the line, temperatures are consistently higher and the monsoon is dominant. North of the line, there are more pronounced seasonal variations, including cold winters. This division directly affects agriculture, energy demand, and infrastructure design across the country.

Q3: Which Indian city lies almost exactly on the Tropic of Cancer?+

Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh, lies almost exactly on the Tropic of Cancer. It is home to an ancient Jantar Mantar observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II, which was used to track the Sun's position, including the summer solstice when the Sun is directly overhead at this latitude.

Q4: Why is the Tropic of Cancer important for solar energy in India?+

The Tropic of Cancer passes through the zone of highest solar insolation in India — the amount of solar radiation reaching the ground. States like Gujarat, Rajasthan, and Madhya Pradesh, which lie along this belt, are ideal locations for large‑scale solar power projects. India's National Solar Mission has concentrated many of its largest installations in this region.

Q5: What happens at the Tropic of Cancer during the summer solstice?+

On 21 June — the summer solstice in the Northern Hemisphere — the Sun is directly overhead at the Tropic of Cancer (23.5°N). This marks the longest day of the year in the Northern Hemisphere and is the point at which the Sun reaches its northernmost position in the sky before appearing to move southward.
