Southeast Asia vs South Asia — Know the Divide

Two regions, 19 countries between them, and a common interview trap: confusing them, or accidentally including Australia. Here's the definitive list.

🇮🇳 South Asia — The Subcontinent

South Asia comprises 8 countries. All eight are members of SAARC (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation). This is the region you live in — India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Maldives, and Afghanistan.

🧭 The Common Mistake — And Why It Matters

Australia is NOT part of Southeast Asia. Australia is sometimes grouped with the "Indo‑Pacific" region in strategic discussions, but geographically and culturally, it is a continent in Oceania, not part of Southeast Asia. A panelist who asks "name the countries of Southeast Asia" is testing whether you will mistakenly include Australia.

Myanmar is the bridge. Myanmar (Burma) sits at the geographic intersection of South Asia and Southeast Asia. It shares a border with both India and Bangladesh (South Asia) and with Thailand and Laos (Southeast Asia). It is a member of ASEAN, not SAARC — placing it firmly in Southeast Asia in modern institutional terms.

The India connection: India's Act East Policy specifically targets Southeast Asia, building on the older Look East Policy. India is a dialogue partner of ASEAN and participates in the East Asia Summit. Understanding the South Asia / Southeast Asia distinction is fundamental to understanding India's foreign policy architecture.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: Name all the countries of South Asia.+

South Asia comprises 8 countries: India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, Sri Lanka, Maldives, and Afghanistan. All eight are members of SAARC.

Q2: Name all the countries of Southeast Asia.+

Southeast Asia comprises 11 countries: Myanmar, Thailand, Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, Philippines, Brunei, and Timor‑Leste. Ten are ASEAN members; Timor‑Leste is an observer.

Q3: Is Australia part of Southeast Asia?+

No. Australia is a continent in Oceania. While it engages deeply with Southeast Asia through ASEAN and is part of the broader "Indo‑Pacific" strategic framework, it is not geographically part of Southeast Asia. This is a common interview trap.

Q4: Is Myanmar in South Asia or Southeast Asia?+

Myanmar is part of Southeast Asia. It is a member of ASEAN, not SAARC. However, it sits at the geographic bridge between the two regions — it borders both India and Bangladesh (South Asia) as well as Thailand and Laos (Southeast Asia).

Q5: Which regional organisations represent South Asia and Southeast Asia?+

South Asia is represented by SAARC (South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation), which includes all 8 South Asian countries. Southeast Asia is represented by ASEAN (Association of Southeast Asian Nations), which includes 10 of the 11 Southeast Asian countries, with Timor‑Leste as an observer. India is a dialogue partner of ASEAN and participates in the East Asia Summit.
