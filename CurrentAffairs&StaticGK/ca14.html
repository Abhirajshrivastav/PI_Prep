⚖️ IMF vs World Bank — The Core Distinction

Though born together, these institutions serve fundamentally different purposes. The simplest way to remember: IMF = Doctor. World Bank = Builder.

Dimension IMF World Bank
Primary Role Global financial stability & crisis management Long‑term development & poverty reduction
What it does Lends to countries facing balance of payments crises; monitors global economy Provides loans, grants, and technical assistance for development projects (schools, roads, health)
Current Head Kristalina Georgieva (Bulgarian economist, since 2019) Ajay Banga (Indian‑American, former Mastercard CEO, since June 2023)
Key Instrument SDR (Special Drawing Right) — a reserve asset IBRD loans, IDA concessional credits for poorest nations
Conditionality Often requires structural reforms (fiscal austerity, etc.) Project‑based conditions; policy advice

Management‑School Insight: The IMF and World Bank are classic examples of principal‑agent dynamics in global governance. Voting power is tied to financial contribution (quotas), not to one‑country‑one‑vote. This means the US and Europe collectively hold disproportionate influence over both institutions — a structural imbalance that BRICS and the New Development Bank (NDB) were explicitly designed to challenge.

💱 The SDR — Special Drawing Right

The Special Drawing Right (SDR) is one of the most frequently misunderstood concepts in global finance — and it appeared directly in one transcript. It is not a currency. It is an international reserve asset created by the IMF in 1969 to supplement member countries' official reserves.

🧠 What the SDR Actually Is

  • The SDR is a basket of five currencies: US Dollar (USD), Euro (EUR), Chinese Renminbi (CNY), Japanese Yen (JPY), and British Pound (GBP).
  • Its value is calculated daily based on weighted exchange rates of these five currencies.
  • Countries can exchange SDRs for freely usable currencies when they face balance of payments difficulties — without conditionality.
  • The Chinese Yuan (Renminbi) was added to the SDR basket in 2016 — a significant milestone in China's push to internationalise its currency.

Why the SDR matters now: In August 2021, the IMF approved a historic $650 billion SDR allocation — the largest in its history — to help countries cope with the economic impact of COVID‑19. India received approximately $17.86 billion worth of SDRs.

🇮🇳 India's Relationship With the IMF & World Bank

India is a founding member of both institutions and has had a complex relationship with them over the decades.

THE 1991 MOMENT

During India's 1991 balance of payments crisis, the IMF provided a $2.2 billion loan — but with conditions. India had to pledge its gold reserves as collateral (a humiliating moment that is seared into Indian policy memory) and implement structural reforms. This crisis directly catalysed the 1991 economic liberalisation under PM Narasimha Rao and FM Manmohan Singh.

PRESENT STATUS

India is now a net creditor to the IMF, not a borrower. It has contributed to the IMF's lending resources. The World Bank continues to finance large infrastructure and development projects in India, though India's reliance has decreased as its own domestic financial capacity has grown.

REFORM ADVOCACY

India consistently advocates for quota reform at the IMF — arguing that voting shares should better reflect the current global economic weight of emerging economies rather than the post‑WWII distribution of power that still favours Europe disproportionately.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: What is the difference between the IMF and the World Bank?+

The IMF is focused on global financial stability — it monitors the global economy and provides short‑term loans to countries facing balance of payments crises. The World Bank is focused on long‑term development — it finances projects like schools, roads, and health systems in developing countries. Think: IMF = doctor for financial emergencies. World Bank = builder for long‑term growth.

Q2: Who currently heads the World Bank, and why is that significant?+

The World Bank is currently led by Ajay Banga, an Indian‑American and IIM Ahmedabad alumnus who previously served as CEO of Mastercard. He took office in June 2023. His appointment is significant because he is the first Indian‑born person to head the institution, reflecting the growing influence of the Indian diaspora in global governance.

Q3: What is the SDR? Is it a currency?+

No, the SDR — Special Drawing Right — is not a currency. It is an international reserve asset created by the IMF in 1969. Its value is based on a basket of five currencies: the US Dollar, Euro, Chinese Renminbi, Japanese Yen, and British Pound. Countries can exchange SDRs for freely usable currencies when needed. The Chinese Yuan was added to the basket in 2016.

Q4: What was India's 1991 crisis and how did the IMF feature in it?+

In 1991, India faced a severe balance of payments crisis — foreign reserves had dropped so low that India could barely cover three weeks of imports. The IMF provided a $2.2 billion loan, but required India to pledge its gold reserves as collateral and implement structural economic reforms. This crisis directly catalysed the 1991 economic liberalisation that transformed India's economy.
