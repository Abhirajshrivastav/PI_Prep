Why the G20 emerged: The 2008 Financial Crisis proved that the G7 alone could not stabilize the global economy. The G20 was elevated precisely because it included the emerging economies that held the reserves and growth needed for a coordinated response.

🇮🇳 India's 2023 G20 Presidency — A Diplomatic Masterclass

India held the G20 Presidency in 2023, hosting the Leaders' Summit in New Delhi in September 2023. The theme was "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" — One Earth, One Family, One Future. It was widely regarded as one of the most ambitious and inclusive presidencies in G20 history.

AFRICAN UNION INCLUSION

India secured a permanent seat for the African Union (AU) in the G20 — a major diplomatic win that elevated the voice of 55 African nations and shifted the global governance architecture. It was compared to the inclusion of the EU in the G20.

DELHI DECLARATION

Despite deep divisions over the Ukraine war, India forged a consensus Delhi Declaration with language that was accepted by both Russia and the Western members — a remarkable feat of diplomatic bridging.

GLOBAL SOUTH SUMMIT

India convened a Voice of the Global South Summit to bring developing nations' concerns — debt, climate finance, food security — to the centre of the G20 agenda.

Management‑School Insight: India's G20 presidency is a case study in coalition‑building and strategic framing. By positioning itself as the voice of the Global South, India bridged the North‑South divide and demonstrated its capacity to shape global agendas without alienating Western partners — a perfect embodiment of strategic autonomy.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: Who are the members of the G7?+

The G7 — Group of Seven — includes the United States, United Kingdom, France, Germany, Italy, Japan, and Canada, plus the European Union as a non‑enumerated member. Russia was suspended in 2014 after the Crimea annexation, reducing the group from G8 to G7.

Q2: What is the main difference between the G7 and the G20?+

The G7 is a club of wealthy, industrialized Western democracies that meet primarily for political and security coordination. The G20 includes all G7 members plus major emerging economies — India, China, Brazil, Russia, South Africa, and others — making it far more representative. The G20 accounts for ~85% of global GDP and 2/3 of the world's population, reflecting the multipolar reality of the 21st century.

Q3: Why was Russia suspended from the G8?+

Russia was suspended from the G8 — which then became the G7 — in 2014 following its annexation of Crimea from Ukraine. The other members deemed the annexation a violation of international law and suspended Russia's participation indefinitely. Russia remains a full member of the G20.

Q4: What was India's major achievement during the 2023 G20 presidency?+

India achieved two major milestones. First, it secured permanent membership for the African Union (AU) in the G20 — a historic diplomatic victory that brought 55 African nations to the table. Second, it brokered the Delhi Declaration consensus on the Ukraine war, getting language accepted by both Russia and the Western powers. The theme was "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam" — One Earth, One Family, One Future.

Q5: Why is the G20 considered more legitimate than the G7 today?+

Because the G7 represents only the wealthy Western world, whose share of global GDP has been declining for decades. The G20 includes emerging economies like India, China, Brazil, Indonesia, and now the African Union — making it far more representative of the global population and economic weight. The G20 was elevated during the 2008 Financial Crisis precisely because the G7 could not solve the problem alone. Global challenges now require global solutions, and the G20 reflects that reality.
