🗣️ Very Common PI Question

“Who is your favourite cabinet minister and why?”

Keep the tone professional, not fan-like. Choose a minister whose work you can genuinely appreciate with a specific, measurable reason.

✅ Safe, Strong Choices

  • S. Jaishankar — diplomacy, articulation
  • Nitin Gadkari — infrastructure delivery
  • Ashwini Vaishnaw — tech & rail modernisation

Always link back to a concrete achievement or policy direction.

🔴

Extremely Important Rule

Ministers can change after reshuffles, elections, or resignations. Recheck the cabinet 2–3 days before your interview, especially these volatile portfolios:

Health Education Railways IT Parliamentary Affairs Civil Aviation

Being up‑to‑date signals thoroughness and genuine interest in governance — a mark of a serious candidate.

Active Recall Practice

Click each question to reveal the model answer. Speak aloud before expanding.

Q1: Who is the current Finance Minister and what is her primary responsibility?+

Model Answer: "Nirmala Sitharaman is the Finance Minister. She is responsible for the Union Budget, fiscal policy, and overall economic management of the country."

Q2: Which minister holds both Railways and Electronics & IT portfolios?+

Model Answer: "Ashwini Vaishnaw holds the Railways portfolio as well as the Electronics and Information Technology portfolio, making him a key figure in infrastructure and digital governance."

Q3: Suggest three professional "favourite minister" choices and justify each briefly.+

Model Answer:
• S. Jaishankar — for his articulate foreign policy and strategic diplomacy.
• Nitin Gadkari — for his record of highway and infrastructure project execution.
• Ashwini Vaishnaw — for modernising railways and advancing India's digital infrastructure.
Keep reasoning factual, not political."

Q4: Why is it critical to recheck the cabinet list just before your interview?+

Model Answer: "Cabinet reshuffles, resignations, or new appointments can happen at any time. Quoting an outdated minister signals poor preparation, while current knowledge shows that you are thorough, attentive to governance, and genuinely up‑to‑date."

Q5: What is a safe approach if you forget a minister's name during the interview?+

Model Answer: "Admit you don't recall the current name with honesty: 'I'm afraid I might not have the latest update on that portfolio right now.' Then pivot to what you do know about the ministry's recent work. It's better to show integrity than guess incorrectly."
