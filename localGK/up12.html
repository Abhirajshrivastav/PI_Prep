🏛️ The Oldest Living City — A 3,000‑Year Continuum

Varanasi, also known as Kashi (the Luminous One) and Banaras, is widely regarded as one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities on earth. Archaeological evidence suggests human settlement dating back to at least 1800 BCE. It is mentioned in the Rigveda, the oldest of the Hindu scriptures, and was already a thriving centre of learning, trade, and religion during the time of the Buddha (6th century BCE).

Unlike many ancient cities that declined or were abandoned, Varanasi has been continuously inhabited and culturally active for over three millennia. It has survived invasions, imperial transitions, and modernisation while retaining its essential identity as the spiritual capital of India. Mark Twain famously wrote: "Banaras is older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend, and looks twice as old as all of them put together."

Interview line: "Varanasi — or Kashi — is considered one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world, with a history stretching back over 3,000 years. It is the spiritual heart of Hindu civilisation and a centre of learning, music, and crafts."

🕉️ The Spiritual Capital of India

Hinduism: Varanasi is the holiest of the seven sacred cities (Sapta Puri) in Hinduism. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas — the most sacred abodes of Shiva. Hindus believe that dying in Varanasi and being cremated on the ghats of the Ganga liberates the soul from the cycle of rebirth (moksha). The Manikarnika Ghat is the most famous cremation ghat, where funeral pyres burn continuously, embodying the city's profound relationship with death and liberation.

Buddhism: Just 10 km from Varanasi lies Sarnath, where Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment — setting in motion the Wheel of Dharma (Dhammachakra Pravartana). This makes Varanasi one of the most important pilgrimage sites for Buddhists worldwide.

Jainism: Varanasi is also sacred to Jains, as it is the birthplace of Parshvanatha, the 23rd Tirthankara.

🌊 The Ghats — The Soul of Varanasi

The 88 ghats along the western bank of the Ganga are the most iconic feature of Varanasi. They are the stages on which the city's daily life, ritual, and commerce unfold. The Dashashwamedh Ghat — where Lord Brahma is believed to have performed the ten‑horse sacrifice — is the main ghat and the site of the famous Ganga Aarti, a spectacular evening ritual of fire, incense, and chanting that draws thousands of visitors every night.

The Assi Ghat — at the confluence of the Assi River and the Ganga — is associated with the poet‑saint Tulsidas and is a centre for morning rituals, yoga, and cultural gatherings. The Tulsi Ghat is named after Tulsidas, who composed the Ramcharitmanas here. Each ghat has its own history, its own community, and its own unique place in the intricate tapestry of the city.

🎓 Banaras Hindu University — The Knowledge Tradition

Founded in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, Banaras Hindu University (BHU) is one of Asia's largest residential universities. It embodies Varanasi's ancient identity as a centre of learning, updated for the modern era. BHU has produced generations of scholars, scientists, artists, and public figures. Its campus — spread over 1,300 acres — is an architectural and cultural landmark, with the Bharat Kala Bhavan museum housing one of India's finest collections of miniature paintings and sculptures.

The Institute of Medical Sciences (IMS‑BHU) is among India's premier medical colleges, and the university's arts and music faculties continue the city's classical traditions.

🎭 Culture, Music, and Crafts

Banarasi Silk: The city is world‑famous for its Banarasi silk sarees, which are GI‑tagged (Geographical Indication). Woven with gold and silver zari work, they are an essential part of Indian weddings and have been produced here for centuries by skilled craftsmen.

Music: Varanasi is a major centre of Hindustani classical music, particularly the Banaras Gharana. It has produced legendary musicians like Bismillah Khan (shehnai maestro), Pandit Ravi Shankar (sitar), and Girija Devi (Thumri). The city's streets and ghats have always resonated with music — from temple bells to classical performances.

Literature: Varanasi has nurtured writers and poets in Sanskrit, Hindi, Urdu, and English, including Munshi Premchand, who lived and wrote here, and Bhartendu Harishchandra, the father of modern Hindi literature.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: Why is Varanasi known as the "oldest living city"?+

Varanasi — also called Kashi — has been continuously inhabited for over 3,000 years, making it one of the world's oldest cities. It is mentioned in the Rigveda and was already a thriving centre of civilisation during the Buddha's time. Its culture, rituals, and traditions have persisted through millennia, giving it a unique status as a living city that is both ancient and vibrantly contemporary.

Q2: What is the religious significance of Varanasi in Hinduism?+

It is the holiest of the Sapta Puri (seven sacred cities). The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is one of the 12 Jyotirlingas. Hindus believe that dying in Varanasi and being cremated on the ghats — especially Manikarnika Ghat — grants moksha, or liberation from the cycle of rebirth. The city is also central to the Ganga's spiritual identity.

Q3: What is the connection between Varanasi and Buddhism?+

Sarnath, located just 10 km from Varanasi, is where Gautama Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment — the Dhammachakra Pravartana (Turning of the Wheel of Dharma). This makes Varanasi one of the four most important Buddhist pilgrimage sites in the world.

Q4: What is Banaras Hindu University (BHU), and why is it significant?+

BHU, founded in 1916 by Pandit Madan Mohan Malaviya, is one of Asia's largest residential universities. It represents Varanasi's deep identity as a centre of learning, encompassing fields from classical arts to modern medicine. Its vast campus, the Bharat Kala Bhavan museum, and institutions like IMS‑BHU make it a premier academic institution in India.

Q5: What are the famous crafts and cultural traditions of Varanasi?+

The most famous craft is the Banarasi silk saree, woven with gold and silver zari — a GI‑tagged product. Musically, the Banaras Gharana is a major school of Hindustani classical music, producing legends like Bismillah Khan, Ravi Shankar, and Girija Devi. The Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat is a globally recognised cultural spectacle.
