Deputy CMs — Keshav Prasad Maurya & Brajesh Pathak

👤 Keshav Prasad Maurya — The OBC Face

Keshav Prasad Maurya

Deputy Chief Minister, Uttar Pradesh (since 2022; previously 2017‑2022)

Keshav Prasad Maurya is the senior of the two Deputy CMs. He was born in a modest farming family in Sirathu, Kaushambi district. His rise through the BJP ranks is closely tied to the party's strategy of cultivating non‑Yadav OBC leadership in Uttar Pradesh. The Maurya/Kushwaha community — traditionally farmers and agriculturalists — is numerically significant in eastern and central UP, and having a Maurya in the top leadership directly addresses caste‑based electoral arithmetic.

He served as the State President of the BJP in Uttar Pradesh from 2016 to 2022, a period during which the party won both the 2017 assembly elections and the 2019 Lok Sabha elections in the state. As a former Union Minister of State for MSME, he also carries central government experience. His portfolios in the UP government include Rural Development, Food Processing, and Panchayati Raj — all critical for the rural, agricultural base that is his political constituency.

Strategic logic: With CM Yogi Adityanath being a Thakur (upper caste), the BJP needed to balance its leadership with strong OBC representation. Keshav Maurya fills that role. The Maurya community, historically aligned with the Samajwadi Party and BSP, has been a key swing constituency that the BJP has successfully courted.

👤 Brajesh Pathak — The Brahmin Face

Brajesh Pathak

Deputy Chief Minister, Uttar Pradesh (since 2022)

Brajesh Pathak is the second Deputy CM, representing the Brahmin community, which constitutes approximately 10‑12% of UP's population and has historically been influential in the state's politics. A lawyer by training, Pathak began his political career in the Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) and later moved to the Congress before joining the BJP — his political journey reflects the shifting allegiances of caste‑based politics in UP.

He was the Law Minister in the first Yogi government (2017‑2022) and was elevated to Deputy CM in 2022. His portfolios include Law, Justice, and Legislative Affairs, along with several other departments. The elevation was widely seen as a message to the Brahmin electorate, which had shown signs of discontent with the BJP over issues like reservation policies and perceived neglect under a Thakur Chief Minister.

Strategic logic: The Brahmins are a traditional BJP support base, but there were murmurs of dissatisfaction before the 2022 elections. Elevating a Brahmin face to Deputy CM was a direct political response to consolidate this crucial vote bank. Pathak's legal background and experience across multiple parties also give him a reputation as an effective administrator.

🧠 Why Two Deputy CMs? — The Political Logic

Uttar Pradesh is one of the few Indian states that has two Deputy Chief Ministers simultaneously. The arrangement is not mandated by the Constitution — the post of Deputy CM itself is not a constitutional office but a political designation — but it serves a clear political purpose:

  • Caste balancing: With a Thakur CM, the BJP needs to reassure both OBCs and Brahmins that their interests are represented at the highest level of the state government. Keshav Maurya (OBC) and Brajesh Pathak (Brahmin) together cover two crucial electoral constituencies.
  • Administrative distribution: Two Deputy CMs can share the administrative burden, each focusing on their assigned portfolios, in a state as large as UP.
  • Internal party management: Both leaders have independent political bases and aspirations. Including them in the top leadership is also a way to manage internal dynamics and prevent factionalism.

Interview insight: "The post of Deputy Chief Minister is not constitutional — it is a political designation. UP has two Deputy CMs: Keshav Prasad Maurya, who represents the OBC community, and Brajesh Pathak, who represents the Brahmin community. With a Thakur Chief Minister, this arrangement is a carefully calibrated caste coalition."

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: Who are the two Deputy Chief Ministers of Uttar Pradesh?+

Keshav Prasad Maurya and Brajesh Pathak. Both are from the BJP and have been serving since the formation of the second Yogi Adityanath government in 2022. Maurya represents the OBC community; Pathak represents the Brahmin community.

Q2: Why does Uttar Pradesh have two Deputy Chief Ministers?+

It is a political arrangement designed to balance caste representation in the state leadership. With CM Yogi Adityanath being a Thakur (upper caste), Keshav Maurya provides representation to the OBC community, and Brajesh Pathak to the Brahmin community. This helps the BJP consolidate its support across multiple caste groups that are crucial to electoral success in UP.

Q3: Is the post of Deputy Chief Minister a constitutional position?+

No. The Constitution of India does not mention the post of Deputy Chief Minister. It is a political designation, not a constitutional office. A Deputy CM is essentially a cabinet minister who has been given the additional title and status of Deputy Chief Minister for political or administrative reasons.

Q4: What is Keshav Prasad Maurya's political background?+

He comes from the Maurya/Kushwaha OBC community. He was the State President of the BJP in UP from 2016 to 2022, a period that saw the party's major electoral victories. He has also served as Union Minister of State for MSME. His political rise is closely tied to the BJP's strategy of building leadership among non‑Yadav OBC castes in Uttar Pradesh.

Q5: What is Brajesh Pathak's background, and why was he elevated to Deputy CM?+

Brajesh Pathak is a lawyer by training and a Brahmin by caste. He started his political career in the BSP, later joined the Congress, and eventually moved to the BJP. He served as Law Minister in the first Yogi government and was elevated to Deputy CM in 2022. His elevation was seen as a move to address potential discontent among Brahmin voters, who are a traditional BJP support base.
