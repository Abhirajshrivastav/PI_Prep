Purvanchal Expressway — 340 km Corridor

The highway that is reshaping eastern Uttar Pradesh — from Lucknow to Ghazipur, this corridor slashes travel time, boosts logistics, and signals a new era for Purvanchal.

Lucknow → Barabanki → Sultanpur → Azamgarh → Mau → Ghazipur

🗺️ Route & Strategic Significance

The Purvanchal Expressway begins at Chand Saray village on the Lucknow–Sultanpur road and terminates at Haidaria village in Ghazipur district, near the UP–Bihar border. It is a fully access‑controlled, 6‑lane expressway, designed to handle speeds up to 120 km/h.

It connects directly to the Lucknow–Agra Expressway and, through it, to the Yamuna Expressway and the Delhi–NCR region. This integration places Ghazipur and the entire Purvanchal region within a single high‑speed corridor reaching all the way to Delhi, bypassing the congested older highways like NH‑731.

What this means for Ghazipur: "My hometown is the eastern terminus of one of India's most significant expressway projects. What was once a 6‑to‑7‑hour drive to Lucknow is now under 4 hours. The expressway physically integrates Purvanchal into the wider economic geography of Uttar Pradesh."

📈 Economic Development

The expressway dramatically improves logistics for agricultural produce from Purvanchal — rice, bananas, mangoes, and vegetables — allowing faster access to markets in Lucknow and Delhi. It lowers transport costs, reduces spoilage, and makes the region more attractive for food processing and manufacturing investment. The UP government has proposed industrial corridors along the expressway to catalyse local employment.

🛩️ Defence Utility

A 3.5‑kilometre stretch of the expressway has been constructed as an emergency airstrip capable of handling Indian Air Force fighter jets, including the Sukhoi‑30 and Mirage‑2000. This is part of India's strategy to create a distributed network of highway airstrips that can be activated during conflict or disaster. It adds a significant dimension of national security to a civilian infrastructure project.

🤝 Social Integration

By cutting travel time, the expressway also connects families, students, and workers. A student from Ghazipur studying at Lucknow University can now make the trip home far more easily. A migrant worker returning from Delhi can reach Ghazipur in a single day rather than an exhausting multi‑day journey. These social dimensions are often overlooked but deeply felt.

⚖️ Addressing the Regional Imbalance

Western Uttar Pradesh — with its proximity to Delhi, the Green Revolution, and earlier industrialisation — has historically been more prosperous than eastern UP. The Purvanchal Expressway is a deliberate policy intervention to bridge this gap. By creating a high‑quality transport backbone, the state hopes to attract investment, encourage local entrepreneurship, and, over time, reduce the distress migration that defines Purvanchal's economy.

It aligns with the central government's PM Gati Shakti National Master Plan, which seeks to integrate road, rail, air, and waterway infrastructure for seamless logistics. The expressway is not just a road — it is a statement that eastern UP will not be left behind.

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: What is the Purvanchal Expressway, and what is its route?+

It is a 340‑kilometre, 6‑lane access‑controlled expressway connecting Chand Saray village (Lucknow) to Haidaria village (Ghazipur), passing through Barabanki, Sultanpur, Azamgarh, and Mau. It was inaugurated in November 2021 and has significantly reduced travel time between Lucknow and Ghazipur from 6‑7 hours to under 4 hours.

Q2: How does the Purvanchal Expressway benefit Ghazipur specifically?+

Ghazipur is the eastern terminus of the expressway. This means the district is now directly connected to Lucknow and, through it, to Delhi‑NCR via a high‑speed corridor. It improves market access for local agricultural produce — rice, bananas, and vegetables — reduces travel time for residents, and opens the possibility of industrial and logistics investment in the area. For a district that has historically been at the periphery of UP's economic development, this is a significant infrastructure intervention.

Q3: What is the strategic or military significance of the expressway?+

A 3.5‑kilometre stretch has been designed as an emergency airstrip capable of handling IAF fighter aircraft. This is part of India's broader strategy of dispersing air assets and creating alternative landing facilities across the country. In peacetime, it is a road; during emergencies, it transforms into a military runway.

Q4: How does the expressway address the regional imbalance between western and eastern UP?+

Western UP benefited from early industrialisation, the Green Revolution, and proximity to Delhi. Purvanchal lagged behind. The expressway is a policy tool to close this gap — it creates a high‑quality transport backbone that lowers logistics costs, attracts investment, and connects the region to larger markets. It aligns with the PM Gati Shakti framework, which views infrastructure as the foundation for inclusive economic growth.
