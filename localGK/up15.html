🌊 The Triveni Sangam — Where Three Rivers Meet

The defining spiritual feature of Prayagraj is the Triveni Sangam — the sacred confluence of three rivers. The Ganga, flowing from the Himalayas, is visibly greenish‑brown with its load of glacial silt. The Yamuna, having travelled from Yamunotri, is darker and bluish‑green. At their meeting point near the fort, the two rivers maintain their distinct colours for a short distance before merging completely. The third river, the Saraswati, is believed to flow invisibly beneath the surface — it is a river of myth and scripture, not geography, and its presence completes the trinity.

The Sangam is the holiest site in Prayagraj and, according to Hindu belief, bathing here during the Kumbh Mela washes away sins and helps liberate the soul from the cycle of rebirth. The spot is marked by a raised platform where priests perform rituals, and pilgrims of every background — from the rural farmer to the international seeker — immerse themselves in the freezing waters during the winter months of the mela.

Interview line: "Prayagraj is defined by the Triveni Sangam — the meeting point of the Ganga, the Yamuna, and the mythical Saraswati. It is the spiritual fulcrum of the Kumbh Mela and, for Hindus, the holiest river confluence in India."

🕉️ The Kumbh Mela — The World's Largest Peaceful Gathering

The Kumbh Mela is not merely a festival; it is the largest congregation of human beings on the planet. In 2019, the Prayagraj Kumbh attracted an estimated 240 million pilgrims over its 49‑day duration. The mela rotates between four locations — Prayagraj, Haridwar, Ujjain, and Nashik — based on specific astrological alignments involving Jupiter (Brihaspati), the Sun (Surya), and the Moon (Chandra).

Astronomical Basis: The timing of the Kumbh Mela is determined by the position of Jupiter in the zodiac, combined with the Sun and Moon's positions. When Jupiter enters Aquarius (Kumbha rashi) and the Sun enters Aries, the Kumbh is held at Prayagraj. This astrological logic is rooted in the ancient Hindu calendar and reflects the deep connection between astronomy and ritual in Indian civilisation.

UNESCO Recognition: In 2017, UNESCO inscribed the Kumbh Mela on its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity. The citation acknowledged it as "the largest peaceful congregation of pilgrims on earth" and recognised its transmission of knowledge, oral traditions, and community identity across generations.

Management‑school perspective: The Kumbh Mela is a phenomenal case study in megacity‑scale temporary infrastructure — a city of tents, sanitation, electricity, transport, and security that is built, operated, and dismantled within weeks. Harvard and other global universities have studied the logistics of the Kumbh as a model of rapid urban deployment.

⚖️ The Legal & Freedom Movement Legacy

Allahabad High Court: Established in 1866, it is one of India's oldest High Courts. Its jurisdiction covers Uttar Pradesh. It has delivered landmark judgments, including the 2010 verdict on the Ayodhya title dispute (later superseded by the Supreme Court). The High Court's bar has produced many of India's most distinguished jurists and political leaders, including Motilal Nehru, the patriarch of the Nehru‑Gandhi family, who practiced law here.

Anand Bhavan: The ancestral home of the Nehru family, donated to the Indian National Congress in 1930. It was the epicentre of the freedom movement in the United Provinces. Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister, was born here in 1889. The building is now a museum preserving the Nehru family's legacy and the history of the independence struggle. Adjacent to it is Swaraj Bhavan (originally Anand Bhavan), the older Nehru residence where Indira Gandhi was born.

Prayagraj in the Freedom Movement: The city was at the heart of the Congress's activities in northern India. It hosted multiple Congress sessions and was a hub of nationalist journalism, with publications like The Leader and The Pioneer operating from here.

🏙️ Allahabad to Prayagraj — The Renaming

In October 2018, the Uttar Pradesh government officially renamed Allahabad to Prayagraj, restoring the city's ancient name. The original name "Prayag" (meaning "place of sacrifice") appears in the Rigveda and is associated with Lord Brahma performing a yajna at the Sangam. The Mughal emperor Akbar renamed it "Ilahabas" in 1575, which later became "Allahabad" — a Persian‑Arabic name meaning "abode of God". The 2018 renaming is part of a broader movement across India to restore indigenous names to cities that were renamed during colonial or Mughal rule.

Interview approach: "Prayagraj, formerly Allahabad, is a city where ancient Hindu pilgrimage, Mughal history, and modern Indian politics all intersect. The renaming in 2018 restored its Vedic name, reflecting a broader cultural reclamation."

🎯 Likely Interview Questions

Q1: What is the Triveni Sangam, and which rivers meet there?+

The Triveni Sangam is the confluence of three rivers in Prayagraj: the Ganga, the Yamuna, and the Saraswati. The Ganga and Yamuna are physically visible — the Ganga is greenish‑brown, the Yamuna darker and bluish — and can be seen meeting at the Sangam. The Saraswati is believed to be a mythical river that flows invisibly beneath the surface. Bathing at this confluence during the Kumbh Mela is considered the holiest of Hindu rituals.

Q2: What is the Kumbh Mela, and how is its timing determined?+

The Kumbh Mela is the world's largest peaceful gathering, rotating among four locations — Prayagraj, Haridwar, Ujjain, and Nashik. Its timing is determined by astrological alignments: specifically, the position of Jupiter (Brihaspati) in the zodiac, combined with the Sun and Moon. When Jupiter enters Aquarius (Kumbha rashi), the Kumbh is held at Prayagraj. In 2017, UNESCO recognised it as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

Q3: What is the historical significance of Prayagraj in India's freedom movement?+

Prayagraj was a central hub of the Indian National Congress. Anand Bhavan, the Nehru family home donated to the Congress, was the epicentre of nationalist activity in the United Provinces. Jawaharlal Nehru was born here, and the city hosted multiple Congress sessions. Motilal Nehru and other prominent leaders practiced law at the Allahabad High Court. The city's newspapers, like The Leader, were instrumental in spreading nationalist ideas.

Q4: Why was Allahabad renamed to Prayagraj?+

The city was renamed in October 2018 to restore its ancient Vedic name. "Prayag" — meaning "place of sacrifice" — appears in the Rigveda and refers to the site where Lord Brahma is believed to have performed a yajna. The Mughal emperor Akbar renamed it "Ilahabas" in 1575, which later became "Allahabad". The 2018 renaming is part of a broader cultural movement across India to reclaim indigenous place names.
